02 — Culture

Culture Transformation

Scoped per engagement
INCLUDED IN TRANSFORMATION / STANDALONE
Ideal For: Organizations experiencing culture failure, persistent underperformance, high attrition, or friction that leadership cannot pinpoint. Also for organizations preparing for significant growth or change who want to define and lock the culture before scaling it.
What This Is

Culture is the sum of every decision made when no one is watching. Most organizations describe the culture they want. Very few have diagnosed the culture they actually have. We do both. We establish an honest, documented baseline — through assessment, interviews, and direct observation — then design what the culture needs to become, and build the system of standards, behaviors, and accountability structures that sustain it without constant intervention from the top.

What Changes

The gap between the values on the wall and the behavior in the hallway closes. The organization has a culture that performs when leadership is not standing over it — and a system to maintain that standard as people change.

What Is Included
  • Culture diagnostic and gap analysis — what the culture actually is versus what leadership believes it is
  • Values alignment and operationalization — translating stated values into daily observable behaviors
  • Organizational accountability architecture — who enforces the standard, how, and with what consequences
  • Manager and leader enablement so mid-level leaders can model and reinforce the culture consistently
  • Culture measurement framework and sustainment plan the organization owns after the engagement ends
Signals This Is Needed
  • High performers are leaving and citing culture as the reason
  • Standards are stated but inconsistently enforced across teams and levels
  • New hires assimilate into the existing culture rather than elevating it
  • Leadership describes the culture differently than the people doing the work
  • Performance varies dramatically by team despite identical resources and direction

05 — Strategy

Strategic Planning & Execution

Scoped per engagement
WORKSHOP THROUGH FULL PLANNING CYCLE
Ideal For: Organizations entering a new planning cycle. Companies preparing for significant growth, a major pivot, or a competitive shift. Any leadership team that has a strategy on paper but consistently struggles to translate it into disciplined execution on the ground.
What This Is

Strategy that lives in a document is not strategy. It is aspiration with a timeline. The gap between a well-designed plan and actual execution is where most organizations lose the most ground — and it is almost always a leadership and accountability problem, not a strategy problem. We facilitate the planning process, build the execution system around it, and hold your organization accountable to the objectives it sets for itself. We do not leave when the plan is written. We stay until execution is real.

What Changes

The organization has a strategy that is understood at every level, translated into clear priorities and ownership, and measured against real accountability checkpoints. Execution becomes a discipline — not a quarterly conversation about why the plan slipped.

Planning Includes
  • Mission, vision, and values alignment — the foundation every other strategic decision is built on
  • Annual and multi-year strategic planning facilitated with the full leadership team
  • OKR and goal framework design — objectives that are clear, measurable, and owned by specific people
  • Priority-setting with explicit tradeoffs — what the organization will and will not do in this cycle
  • Written strategic framework document the organization uses as a live operating reference, not a filed report
Execution System Includes
  • Execution rhythm and operating cadence — the meeting structures and review cycles that maintain momentum
  • Accountability architecture — who reviews progress, how often, with what authority to course correct
  • Milestone and checkpoint framework so leadership knows early when execution is drifting from plan
  • Mid-cycle strategy review facilitation to assess results and make real-time adjustments before the year is lost
  • Annual after-action review process that builds organizational learning into the next planning cycle

07 — AI Operations

Workflow Modernization & AI Integration

Scoped per engagement
INCLUDED IN AI ADVISORY / STANDALONE
Ideal For: Organizations where the biggest productivity gains are buried in repetitive workflows, manual reporting, or information transfer that consumes leadership time without producing leadership value. Operations teams preparing for AI-enabled workflow redesign.
What This Is

Most AI adoption fails at the workflow level — not because the technology does not work, but because it was layered onto processes that were already broken rather than redesigned around what AI actually does well. We map your highest-friction workflows, identify precisely where AI creates leverage, redesign the process around that leverage, and build the training and governance that makes the change stick. The goal is not to automate everything. The goal is to free leadership time for the work only leaders can do.

What Changes

Time-consuming manual processes that consumed leadership bandwidth are redesigned and partially automated. Leaders spend more time leading and less time managing information. The organization operates faster without adding headcount.

What Is Included
  • Current state workflow mapping — every high-friction process documented, timed, and evaluated for AI leverage
  • AI opportunity identification with specific tool recommendations for each workflow redesign target
  • Redesigned workflow documentation with human and AI responsibilities clearly defined at every step
  • Training design so every person in the workflow understands their role in the new process
  • 30-day adoption review to assess uptake, address friction, and course correct before habits calcify
Common Workflow Targets
  • Reporting and briefing preparation that consumes staff time without improving the quality of decisions
  • Communication drafting and distribution that leadership reviews but rarely authors from scratch
  • Data aggregation and synthesis that delays decision-making by days instead of hours
  • Document review, summarization, and routing that creates bottlenecks in approval chains
  • Training material development and after-action documentation that gets delayed or skipped entirely
08 — AI Governance

AI Governance & Responsible Use Frameworks

Scoped per engagement
INCLUDED IN AI ADVISORY / STANDALONE
Ideal For: Organizations already using AI who have not yet established formal governance. Commands navigating DoD AI policy requirements. Companies whose legal, compliance, or HR teams are pressing for AI policy before incidents force the issue. Any organization where AI decisions are being made at the wrong level.
What This Is

The organizations that get hurt by AI are not the ones that move too slowly. They are the ones that move without governance — where individual contributors are making consequential AI decisions that should be owned by leadership. We build the governance frameworks, decision authority matrices, acceptable use policies, and monitoring protocols that keep humans in command at every level of the organization. Responsible adoption is not a constraint on speed. It is what makes speed sustainable.

What Changes

Leadership owns AI decisions at the appropriate level. Risk is visible before incidents occur. The organization can expand AI adoption confidently because the governance infrastructure is already in place to support it.

What Is Included
  • AI governance framework — principles, decision authorities, and accountability structure from the top down
  • Acceptable use policy covering approved tools, prohibited applications, data handling, and output verification
  • Decision authority matrix — what AI decisions require human review, at what level, with what documentation
  • Required training standards and competency verification for every role using AI in the organization
  • Tool approval and sunset process — how new tools get evaluated, approved, monitored, and retired
Governance Protects Against
  • AI outputs treated as authoritative without human verification at consequential decision points
  • Data privacy violations from tools ingesting sensitive organizational or client information
  • Accountability gaps when AI-assisted decisions produce adverse outcomes and no one owns the result
  • Tool proliferation where individual teams adopt AI without coordination, security review, or shared standards
  • Regulatory and compliance exposure as AI policy requirements expand across sectors and jurisdictions
