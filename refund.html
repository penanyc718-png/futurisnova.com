Engagement & Cancellation Policy

Futuris Nova LLC • Last Updated: March 2026 • Effective: March 2026

This policy governs all consulting, coaching, advisory, and training engagements with Futuris Nova LLC. Specific terms are documented in a Statement of Work (SOW) or services agreement. This policy supplements the SOW and applies where the SOW is silent.

1. Engagement Confirmation

An engagement is confirmed when both parties have executed a written SOW or services agreement. No verbal agreements, email exchanges, or preliminary conversations constitute a binding engagement. Futuris Nova LLC reserves the right to decline any engagement at its sole discretion.

2. Project-Based Engagements

  • Deposit — a deposit as specified in the SOW is required prior to commencement of work and will be credited against the total engagement fee
  • Milestone billing — remaining fees are billed per the schedule in the SOW, typically tied to defined milestones or phases
  • Scope changes — any material change to scope requires a written amendment to the SOW and may affect timeline and fees
  • Completion — the engagement is complete upon delivery of all SOW-defined deliverables and acceptance by the client

3. Retained Advisory Engagements

  • Billing — retained engagements are billed monthly in advance on the first of each month
  • Minimum term — retained engagements have a minimum term as specified in the SOW, typically three months
  • Unused capacity — unused advisory hours within a billing period do not carry forward
  • Rate adjustments — Futuris Nova LLC may adjust retained rates with 30 days written notice

4. Cancellation by Client

  • Before work commences — if cancellation occurs more than 10 business days before the scheduled start date and before work has begun, the deposit may be refunded at Futuris Nova LLC's discretion, less any reasonable preparation costs already incurred
  • After work commences — project-based — the client is responsible for all fees for work completed through the cancellation date, plus a cancellation fee equal to 25% of the remaining engagement value
  • After work commences — retained — the client is responsible for the current month's fee plus any remaining fees under the minimum term, with 30 days written notice required
  • Rescheduling — session or workshop rescheduling requires 5 business days advance notice; less than 5 days notice may incur a rescheduling fee as specified in the SOW

5. Cancellation by Futuris Nova LLC

Futuris Nova LLC reserves the right to cancel or suspend an engagement in cases of non-payment, material breach by the client, force majeure, or at our sole discretion with 30 days written notice and pro-rata refund of prepaid fees for undelivered services.

6. Refund Conditions

  • Cancellation by Futuris Nova LLC — pro-rata refund of prepaid fees for services not yet delivered
  • Material failure to deliver SOW-defined deliverables — refund proportional to undelivered work after good-faith resolution attempt
  • Force majeure — pro-rata refund for services not delivered, with no cancellation fee applied

Completed work, delivered sessions, and advisory time already rendered are not subject to refund. Futuris Nova LLC does not offer refunds based on satisfaction with outcomes, as outcomes depend on factors including client implementation and organizational conditions beyond our control.

7. Intellectual Property Upon Termination

  • Client retains ownership of all deliverables paid for in full
  • Futuris Nova LLC retains all underlying methodologies, frameworks, tools, and intellectual property used to create deliverables
  • Partial deliverables for which full payment has not been received remain the property of Futuris Nova LLC until payment is complete

8. Dispute Resolution

Any dispute regarding fees, deliverables, or this policy shall first be addressed through good-faith negotiation. If resolution is not achieved within 30 days, disputes shall be resolved through binding arbitration in California per the Terms and Conditions. This policy is governed by California law.
