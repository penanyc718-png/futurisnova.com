18 Prompts Built for Leaders

How Persona Prompts Work

These prompts assign AI a specific role — a skeptical subordinate, a board member, the frontline person your decision actually lands on. You interact with AI as if it were that person. Use them to hear the pushback before you stand in front of the room, stress-test plans before you commit resources, and see your leadership from angles that are nearly impossible to access when you are inside the organization. The more specific your context, the sharper the output.

Leadership Development
Team Culture Assessment
Surface your team's real cultural strengths and the gaps quietly limiting performance — before they become problems you are managing instead of preventing.
"Analyze the following team description and identify the top three cultural strengths and the top three cultural gaps that may be limiting performance. Then provide specific, actionable steps to address each gap. Be direct and prioritize by impact. Team context: [describe your team size, mission, current performance challenges, and any recurring friction you observe]."
Best for: Team leads, commanders, executives
Decision Making
Structured Decision Brief
Stop making decisions alone in your head. Generate a full decision brief with three courses of action, tradeoffs, and a recommended path — in minutes.
"I need to make a decision about [describe the decision]. The key stakeholders affected are [list them]. The constraints I am working within are [time, resources, authority level]. The risk I am most concerned about is [describe it]. My organizational mission or goal is [describe it]. Generate a structured decision brief with three distinct courses of action, pros and cons for each, risk level, and a recommendation with rationale."
Best for: Senior leaders, commanders, executives
AI Strategy
AI Readiness Self-Assessment
Get an honest AI readiness score for your organization before investing a dollar — and a prioritized starting point that actually fits your context.
"Help me assess my organization's readiness to adopt AI tools. My organization type is [military unit / company / government agency / nonprofit]. We currently use [describe current technology tools]. Our team's general comfort with technology is [low / medium / high]. Our primary workflow challenges are [describe 2-3 specific pain points]. Our most important outcome in the next 12 months is [describe it]. Give me an AI readiness assessment with a 1-10 score and rationale, and a prioritized list of three AI use cases we should explore first with implementation notes for each."
Best for: Leaders beginning AI exploration
Executive Coaching
Leadership Feedback Interpreter
Turn feedback you received into a clear action plan — with an honest interpretation of what it is really saying about your leadership impact on others.
"I received the following feedback about my leadership: [paste or describe the feedback as specifically as possible]. Help me: 1) Interpret what this feedback is really saying about my leadership impact and how others experience me. 2) Identify the root behavior or pattern that is driving this perception. 3) Give me three specific, observable actions I can take in the next 30 days to begin addressing it. Be direct and honest — I need clarity, not comfort."
Best for: Any leader seeking honest growth
Communication
Leadership Message Builder
Build a clear, direct leadership message for any audience — formation, staff meeting, all-hands, or email — tailored to your intent and their actual needs.
"Help me write a leadership message to my team about [topic or situation]. Here is the context: [describe what is happening and why it matters]. My intent is [what I want them to understand, feel, or do after hearing this]. The tone should be [direct / steady / motivational / urgent / candid — choose one]. My audience is [describe your team: their role, experience level, current mindset]. Draft a 3-paragraph message I can deliver in a formation, meeting, or email. No corporate filler. Lead with the point."
Best for: Leaders at any level
Strategy and Execution
Organizational Gap Analyzer
Identify the real root cause behind persistent underperformance — not the symptoms — and get a phased 90-day plan to close the gap.
"My organization is consistently underperforming. Our mission is [describe it]. Our current performance issues include [list 2-3 specific, observable problems]. The feedback I consistently hear from my team is [describe it]. Leadership actions I have already tried include [describe what has been attempted]. Constraints I am working within: [resources, authority, timeline]. Identify the most likely root cause of our performance gap — not the symptoms — and give me a phased 90-day action plan to address it with specific milestones."
Best for: Senior leaders and commanders
AI Strategy
AI Tool Evaluator
Evaluate any AI tool against your specific organizational needs before committing time or budget — with a structured risk and value assessment.
"I am evaluating the following AI tool for potential adoption: [name the tool]. My organization's primary use case would be [describe what problem we want to solve]. My team size is [number] and technical proficiency is [low / medium / high]. My biggest concerns are [list 2-3: cost, security, learning curve, accuracy, governance, etc.]. Evaluate this tool across five dimensions: capability fit, ease of adoption, data security considerations, leadership governance requirements, and total cost of ownership. Give me a structured go/no-go recommendation with conditions."
Best for: Leaders evaluating AI investments
Leadership Development
Subordinate Leader Developer
Build a personalized 60-day development plan for a leader on your team — based on their actual gaps, not a generic template.
"I am developing a subordinate leader and need a personalized plan. Here is their profile: Current role: [describe]. Time in role: [duration]. Key strengths I observe: [list 2-3]. Primary development gaps: [list 2-3 specific, observable behaviors]. Upcoming challenges or assignments: [describe]. Their career goals: [describe if known]. Build a 60-day leader development plan with specific activities, feedback mechanisms, and accountability checkpoints I can use to track progress and hold them to the standard."
Best for: Team leaders, commanders, managers
Decision Making
Pre-Mortem Analysis
Run a pre-mortem before executing any major initiative — identify the failure modes before they happen and build a prevention plan before resources are committed.
"I am about to execute the following plan or initiative: [describe it in detail including the goal, key stakeholders, timeline, and resources committed]. Run a pre-mortem analysis. Assume it is 6 months from now and the initiative has failed. List the 5 most likely reasons it failed — ranked by probability and impact. For each failure mode, give me one specific mitigation action I can take now before execution begins. Be direct and assume I can handle honest analysis."
Best for: Leaders before major decisions or launches
Coaching — Veteran Transition
Career Transition Navigator
Translate your military experience into civilian career language — and identify the roles where your leadership translates with the greatest impact.
"I am a transitioning military leader and need help positioning my experience in the civilian job market. My rank and MOS or specialty: [describe]. Years of service: [number]. Most significant leadership accomplishments: [list 3-4 with scale and outcome]. Degrees or certifications: [list]. Target industry or role type: [describe if known, or say unsure]. Translate my military background into a civilian leadership narrative, identify three career paths where my experience translates most directly, and give me a list of the civilian equivalents for my key competencies."
Best for: Transitioning veterans and NCOs
AI Strategy
AI Governance Framework Builder
Build a responsible AI governance framework for your organization — so leaders stay in command of every AI tool that enters the workflow.
"I need to build an AI governance framework for my organization. Context: [organization type, size, and primary mission]. AI tools currently in use or under consideration: [list them]. My primary concerns are [choose from: data privacy, accuracy/hallucination, security, bias, accountability, workforce impact — list your top 3]. Build a practical AI governance framework that includes: principles for responsible use, a decision authority matrix for approving new tools, required training standards for users, and a process for reviewing and retiring tools that no longer meet our standards."
Best for: Senior leaders implementing AI programs
Strategy and Execution
Strategic Planning Accelerator
Compress your strategic planning cycle without losing rigor — generate a structured framework in one session your team can build from immediately.
"Help me build a strategic framework for my organization. Context: Organization type and mission: [describe]. Current state: [describe where we are — strengths and problems]. Desired end state in 12 months: [describe what success looks like]. Resources available: [people, budget, time]. Key external factors affecting us: [describe 2-3]. Build a one-page strategic framework including: a mission-aligned goal statement, three strategic priorities with rationale, key performance indicators for each priority, the top three risks to success and mitigation approaches, and the single most important decision I need to make in the next 30 days."
Best for: Leaders at every level, every sector
◇ Persona — Resistance Testing
The Skeptical Subordinate
Hear the real pushback before you stand in front of the room. Put AI in the role of your most resistant, experienced team member — and let them push back hard.
"I want you to play the role of a skeptical, experienced team member who is resistant to change and has seen many leadership initiatives fail. Your character: [describe — e.g., a 12-year NCO who has watched multiple commanders cycle through, or a mid-level manager who has been through three restructures in four years]. I am about to deliver the following message or announce the following change: [describe your message or decision in full]. Respond to my message the way this person would — with their real doubts, their unspoken objections, and the questions they would never ask me directly. Do not hold back. I need to hear the resistance before I stand in front of the room."
Best for: Leaders before major messages or change announcements
◇ Persona — Outside Perspective
The Outside Evaluator
Get an unfiltered outside assessment of your organization — the way a seasoned external consultant would deliver it, without politics, loyalty, or the need to protect anyone's feelings.
"You are an experienced organizational consultant who has worked with hundreds of military units, corporate teams, and government agencies. You are known for being direct, accurate, and politically neutral. I am going to describe my organization, and I want you to evaluate it as if you were brought in for the first time with no relationship with anyone inside it. Organization type and mission: [describe]. Team size and structure: [describe]. Current performance challenges: [describe honestly]. What leadership has tried: [describe]. What people inside say privately: [describe if you know]. Give me your honest outside assessment — what you would tell the senior leader in a private debrief with nothing held back."
Best for: Leaders who want a reality check without internal politics
◇ Persona — Decision Stress Test
The Devil's Advocate
Before you commit — hear the strongest possible argument against your decision from someone who is not trying to help you feel good about it.
"You are a brilliant, well-informed devil's advocate. Your job is to argue the strongest possible case against the decision or plan I am about to share. You are not being contrarian — you genuinely believe there is a better way and you have the data and logic to back it up. Do not soften your argument. Do not give me a balanced view. Give me the strongest case against my position. My decision or plan: [describe it in full, including your reasoning and the outcome you expect]. Now argue against it — hard. Then, after your argument, tell me the single most important thing I should address or reconsider before I commit."
Best for: Leaders before committing to major decisions or plans
◇ Persona — Advisory
The Ideal Mentor
Access the perspective of a senior leader with thirty years of relevant experience — one who tells you the truth and has nothing to prove or protect.
"You are a senior leader with 30+ years of experience in [military leadership / executive corporate leadership / government service — choose what fits]. You have led organizations through everything — high performance, crisis, culture failure, rapid growth, and major transitions. You are known for being direct, honest, and deeply invested in the development of the people you mentor. You do not sugarcoat. You do not validate poor decisions. Here is my situation: [describe your challenge, decision, or development question in full]. Respond the way a trusted mentor with this background would — give me your honest perspective, your experience-based assessment, and one thing you wish someone had told you when you were in a similar position."
Best for: Leaders facing development questions, transitions, or complex challenges
◇ Persona — Impact Testing
The Frontline Voice
Before you issue a directive — hear it through the eyes of the person it actually lands on. The one who rarely gets asked. The one who will live with your decision long after the briefing ends.
"You are a frontline team member — [describe the role: e.g., a junior NCO with 4 years of service, or an entry-level employee in their second year]. You are not in leadership. You do not have context for the decisions made above you. You experience the organization through what actually happens to you day to day. I am about to share a leadership decision or policy that affects you directly: [describe the decision or policy in full]. Respond the way this person would — tell me what they understand, what they do not understand, how this decision feels to them, what they will say to their peers after the briefing, and what question they wish they could ask but never would."
Best for: Leaders before issuing policies, directives, or major organizational decisions
◇ Persona — Executive Lens
The Board Member
Evaluate your proposal, strategy, or briefing through the lens of the person who has to vote on it — before you walk into the room where the decision gets made.
"You are a senior board member reviewing the following proposal, strategy, or communication from a leader inside the organization. You have seen hundreds of proposals. You are not easily impressed. You ask hard questions about return on investment, risk, execution credibility, and alignment with organizational priorities. You hold leaders accountable for what they say they will deliver. Here is what I am presenting to you: [describe your proposal, plan, or strategy in full — include the goal, the approach, the investment required, and the outcome you are projecting]. Review it the way a skeptical but fair board member would. Tell me what you find compelling, what you find weak, and the three questions you would ask before you vote on it."
Best for: Leaders preparing proposals, funding requests, or executive briefings

Leadership Insights

Practical Thinking. Drawn from the Work.

Not theory. Not borrowed frameworks. Observations from twenty years of leading and consulting — on leadership, AI, culture, and what actually separates organizations that execute from organizations that talk about executing.

Leadership

The Gap Between Authority and Influence

Rank gives you authority. It does not give you influence. The leaders who consistently produce results are the ones who understand the difference — and invest in building both. Authority commands compliance. Influence commands commitment. Only one of those builds an organization that performs when you are not in the room.

AI Strategy

Why Most AI Initiatives Fail Before They Start

Most organizations approach AI adoption backwards. They invest in tools before investing in readiness. They expect AI to fix problems that are fundamentally leadership and culture problems. The organizations that succeed treat it as a leadership challenge first — and a technology challenge second.

Organizational Culture

Culture Is Built in What You Tolerate

Every leader talks about the culture they want to build. But culture is not built in what you reward. It is built in what you tolerate. The gap between the standards you proclaim and the behaviors you accept is exactly the size of your culture problem. Close that gap first — before investing in anything else.

Leadership Development

The Problem with Leadership Training

Most leadership training produces knowledge, not capability. There is a significant difference. Capability is what a leader can do under pressure, in ambiguous conditions, with incomplete information. You cannot build that in a classroom. You build it through deliberate practice, honest feedback, and accountability to outcomes over time.

AI and Leadership

AI Will Not Replace Leaders. It Will Expose Them.

The leaders who struggle most with AI are not the ones who lack technical knowledge. They are the ones whose value was always tied to information access rather than judgment. AI democratizes information. What it cannot replace is the human capacity to make wise decisions, build trust, and lead people through uncertainty.

Execution

Strategy Fails at the Transition from Plan to Action

Most strategic plans are sound. Most execution is not. The gap is almost always in the translation — the moment when a well-designed plan meets the friction of real operations, real people, and real constraints. Bridging that gap requires leaders who understand both the strategy and the ground truth of what their organizations can actually do.
