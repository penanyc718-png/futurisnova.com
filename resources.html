Leadership Persona Builder

Chapter Two directs every reader to build their AI leadership persona before using any other prompt in the book. This is the Master Prompt — ready to copy and paste directly into Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, or any AI platform you use.

Your persona tells the AI who you are, how you think, and what kind of outputs serve your leadership style. Once you build it, paste it at the start of any important conversation. The AI calibrates to you — not to a generic user. This is the single most important thing you can do before using any other resource on this page.

Master Prompt — Leadership Persona Builder
You are my AI leadership advisor and strategic thinking partner. I want you to learn who I am so that every response you give me is calibrated to my specific leadership context, communication style, and goals. Here is my profile. Read it carefully and confirm you understand it before we begin: MY ROLE: [Describe your position — leader, manager, executive, commander, etc.] MY ORGANIZATION: [Describe your organization — type, size, mission, industry] MY LEADERSHIP STYLE: [How do you lead — directive, collaborative, coaching-oriented, mission-focused, etc.] MY COMMUNICATION PREFERENCE: [How do you like information — direct and brief, detailed and analytical, narrative, visual, etc.] MY CURRENT PRIORITIES: [What are the top 2-3 things you are working on right now] MY BIGGEST CHALLENGE: [What is the hardest thing you are dealing with right now] MY GOALS: [What outcomes are you trying to produce in the next 90 days] INSTRUCTIONS FOR HOW TO WORK WITH ME: — Always address me as a senior leader who can handle direct, unfiltered feedback — When I ask for analysis, give me your honest assessment first, then supporting detail — When I ask for options, give me 3 maximum with a clear recommendation — When I ask you to draft something, match my voice — [describe your writing voice] — Flag when you think I am asking the wrong question — Challenge my assumptions when you have information I may not have considered — Never pad responses with unnecessary caveats or disclaimers — Format outputs for action, not for reading — use bullets, numbered lists, and headers Confirm you have read and understood my profile. Then ask me one clarifying question to better calibrate to my leadership context.

Appendix A — Selected Prompts

Prompt Library

A selection of the most-used prompts from the book, ready to copy and use immediately. Each prompt is designed for a specific leadership context and tested across multiple AI platforms.

These prompts are starting points, not scripts. Customize them with your specific context, organization, and objectives for best results. The more specific you are, the more useful the output.

Chapter Three
After-Action Review Facilitator
You are facilitating an After-Action Review (AAR) for the following event: EVENT: [Describe the event, mission, or project] DATE/TIMEFRAME: [When did it occur] PARTICIPANTS: [Who was involved] INTENDED OUTCOME: [What were we trying to accomplish] ACTUAL OUTCOME: [What actually happened] Facilitate a structured AAR in sequence: 1. What was supposed to happen? 2. What actually happened? What were the key deviations? 3. Why did it happen? Root causes — not symptoms. 4. What do we sustain? What worked and why? 5. What do we improve? Specific changes for next time? Probe for specificity. Do not accept vague answers. Push for facts, examples, and root causes. Generate a written AAR summary with clear sustain and improve items, assigned owners, and follow-up timelines.
Works best in Claude or ChatGPT
Chapter Four
Decision Brief Generator
I need to make a decision and I want you to help me think through it rigorously. DECISION REQUIRED: [Describe the decision] DECISION DEADLINE: [When does this need to be decided] KEY STAKEHOLDERS: [Who is affected] CONSTRAINTS: [Budget, time, policy, personnel, or other limitations] AVAILABLE INFORMATION: [What do you know right now] WHAT YOU DO NOT KNOW: [What information is missing] Structure your analysis: 1. Restate the decision in precise terms 2. Identify 3 courses of action including the option to do nothing 3. For each COA: advantages, disadvantages, risks, resource requirements 4. Identify what additional information would change the analysis 5. Make a recommendation with your reasoning 6. Identify the first action required to execute Be direct. I need analysis I can act on.
Works best in Claude or Gemini
Chapter Five
Team Performance Diagnostic
I want to diagnose the performance of a team I lead. TEAM DESCRIPTION: [Size, function, how long it has existed] CURRENT PERFORMANCE: [What is the team producing now] DESIRED PERFORMANCE: [What should the team be producing] WHAT IS WORKING: [What aspects of the team are strong] WHAT IS NOT WORKING: [Where are the visible breakdowns] MY HYPOTHESIS: [What do I currently believe is the root cause] Analyze and: 1. Identify most likely root causes — distinguish people, process, resource, and leadership problems 2. Challenge my hypothesis with alternative explanations 3. Recommend the 3 highest-leverage interventions in the next 30 days 4. Identify what I might be contributing to the problem as the leader 5. Suggest 3 diagnostic questions I should ask team members directly Do not tell me what I want to hear. Tell me what the evidence suggests.
Works best in Claude
Chapter Six
Strategic Communication Drafter
I need to communicate the following to my organization. MESSAGE TOPIC: [What needs to be communicated] AUDIENCE: [Who will receive this — their level, role, and what they care about] OBJECTIVE: [What do I want the audience to think, feel, or do after receiving this] CURRENT SITUATION: [Context the audience needs] KEY POINTS: [The 3-5 things that must be in this message] TONE: [Formal/informal, urgent/informational, motivational/directive] FORMAT: [Email, speech, briefing, memo, video script, etc.] LENGTH: [Approximate length or time] Draft following these principles: — Lead with what matters most, not with background — Be specific — avoid vague language — End with a clear call to action After the draft, give me your honest assessment: Is this message likely to land the way I intend? What might the audience hear that I do not mean to say?
Works best in Claude or ChatGPT
Chapter Seven
Culture Gap Analyzer
I want to analyze the gap between the culture my organization says it has and the culture it actually has. STATED VALUES: [List the official values or culture statements] OBSERVED BEHAVIORS: [What behaviors do you actually see regularly] WHAT GETS REWARDED: [What behaviors lead to recognition or promotion] WHAT GETS TOLERATED: [What behaviors persist without consequence] WHAT GETS PUNISHED: [What behaviors lead to negative consequences] RECENT CRITICAL INCIDENTS: [Events that revealed something about the true culture] Analyze: 1. Where stated values are genuinely lived — specific evidence 2. Where the largest gaps exist between stated and actual culture 3. What the reward and punishment patterns reveal about what the organization actually values 4. The most important cultural shift needed 5. 3 specific leadership behaviors I can change in the next 30 days to begin closing the gap Culture is what leaders do, not what they say. Be direct about what the evidence suggests about leadership's role in the current culture.
Works best in Claude
Chapter Eight
AI Implementation Readiness Check
I want to assess whether my organization is ready to implement AI tools and where to start. ORGANIZATION TYPE: [Sector, size, mission] CURRENT TECH STACK: [What tools and systems does the organization use] LEADERSHIP AI FLUENCY: [Not at all / basic awareness / some experience / advanced] TEAM AI FLUENCY: [Same question for the broader team] BIGGEST OPERATIONAL PAIN POINTS: [Where does the organization lose the most time or quality] CURRENT DATA PRACTICES: [How is data collected, stored, and used] RISK TOLERANCE: [How much experimentation and failure can the organization absorb] Assess readiness across five dimensions: 1. Leadership readiness 2. Workforce readiness 3. Data readiness 4. Process readiness 5. Governance readiness For each dimension rate readiness as low, medium, or high and explain why. Then recommend the 3 highest-impact starting points for AI adoption given where this organization actually is — not where it wishes it was.
Works best in Claude or ChatGPT
